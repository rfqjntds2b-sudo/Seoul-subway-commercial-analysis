Situation (Current Market and Services)
Details: Currently, domestic local map platforms (Naver Maps, KakaoMap, etc.) provide real-time, comprehensive services such as restaurant information, star ratings, and the fastest public transit routes. Users routinely search these platforms to discover hot places to visit on Friday nights or weekends.
Key Takeaway: Existing platforms have already reached their peak in terms of 'information volume' and 'transit convenience.'

Complication (Hidden Pain Points of Users)
Details: However, as this information has become highly democratized, users arriving at recommended hot spots (e.g., Gangnam, Hongdae, etc.) are often met with unpleasant experiences: suffocating crowds, extreme public transit congestion, and endless restaurant waiting times. Recently, young users (2030 generation) have shown a clear preference for pleasant, under-the-radar commercial districts that are relatively uncrowded yet rich in activities (the "congestion game winners"). Nevertheless, current map apps lack a precise index that fuses and compares 'crowd density (Cost)' and 'commercial infrastructure (Benefit).'
Key Takeaway: Simply recommending popular hotspots intensifies user concentration, degrading user experience (UX). There is a lack of exploration criteria that simultaneously consider 'pleasantness' and 'commercial infrastructure.'

Question (Key Challenge We Need to Solve)
Details: "How can we merge public transit alighting population (congestion) and shop density data within map apps to intuitively suggest [Congestion Cost-Benefit (C/P) Commercial Zones] that are less crowded yet offer rich options?"

Answer (Our Final Proposal and Solution)
Details: We propose introducing a 'Congestion Cost-Benefit (C/P) Index' feature, combining Seoul's public transit (subway/bus) alighting flow and business directory data through spatial matching (Haversine 500m radius scans). By implementing a new 'Congestion Game Layer' within map apps, we can provide users with a comfortable Friday night out while offering map platforms increased user retention and maximized business value (expanding B2B advertising models) from commercial data.
